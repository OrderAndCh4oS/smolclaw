# Salable Marketing Content Strategy

This document outlines the comprehensive 12-week content marketing strategy for Salable, covering goals, target audiences, content pillars, and distribution channels.

## Goals and Objectives

### Primary Goals

**Lead Generation**
Drive qualified traffic to the Salable platform through educational content that demonstrates expertise in subscription billing. Target developers and founders actively researching billing solutions, capturing them at the consideration stage of their buying journey.

**Thought Leadership**
Position Salable as the authoritative voice on subscription pricing strategy, marketplace monetisation, and billing operations. Build trust through deep, practical content that goes beyond surface-level advice.

**SEO and Organic Growth**
Establish topical authority in key search verticals: SaaS pricing, subscription billing, Trello Power-Up monetisation, Miro plugin billing, and usage-based pricing. Target long-tail keywords with commercial intent.

## Target Audience Personas

### SaaS Founder

**Profile**: Building or scaling a subscription-based software business. Makes strategic decisions about pricing, technology stack, and go-to-market. Often technical but increasingly focused on business outcomes.

**Pain Points**:

- Pricing strategy feels like guesswork
- Billing complexity distracts from core product development
- Worried about leaving revenue on the table with wrong pricing model
- Needs to move fast but billing mistakes are costly

**Content Needs**: Strategic guidance on pricing models, case studies of successful pricing strategies, practical implementation guides that respect their time.

**Search Behaviour**: "SaaS pricing strategy", "per-seat vs usage-based pricing", "subscription billing best practices"

---

### Marketplace Developer

**Profile**: Building apps, plugins, or integrations for platforms like Trello, Miro, Notion, or Slack. Often a solo developer or small team. Wants to monetise their creation but unfamiliar with billing infrastructure.

**Pain Points**:

- Platform marketplace guidelines are confusing
- No clear path from free app to paid product
- Worried about user backlash when introducing pricing
- Lacks infrastructure to handle payments professionally

**Content Needs**: Platform-specific monetisation guides, pricing benchmarks for their marketplace, step-by-step billing setup tutorials.

**Search Behaviour**: "Trello Power-Up pricing", "how to charge for Miro plugin", "monetise Notion integration"

---

### Engineering Lead

**Profile**: Responsible for technical decisions about billing integration. Evaluates build vs buy, manages billing system reliability, and ensures the integration doesn't become a maintenance burden.

**Pain Points**:

- Billing code is notoriously error-prone
- Edge cases multiply faster than expected
- Debugging payment issues requires Stripe expertise
- Fear of breaking billing and losing revenue

**Content Needs**: Technical deep-dives on billing architecture, comparison of DIY vs platform approaches, webhook handling best practices, testing strategies.

**Search Behaviour**: "Stripe subscription billing integration", "webhook reliability patterns", "billing system testing"

---

### Product Manager

**Profile**: Owns pricing strategy and feature packaging decisions. Works between business objectives and technical constraints. Needs to justify pricing changes with data.

**Pain Points**:

- Lacks tools to experiment with pricing
- Feature gating is manual and error-prone
- No visibility into how pricing changes affect behaviour
- Pressure to increase revenue without alienating users

**Content Needs**: Pricing experimentation frameworks, entitlement management strategies, competitive pricing analysis, feature packaging best practices.

**Search Behaviour**: "SaaS feature gating", "pricing tier optimization", "how to raise SaaS prices"

## Content Pillars

Each piece of content maps to one of four strategic pillars, ensuring comprehensive coverage of topics that matter to our audience.

### Pricing Mastery

Strategic content about choosing, implementing, and optimising pricing models. Covers flat-rate, per-seat, usage-based, tiered, and hybrid approaches. Helps readers understand when each model fits and how to execute it.

**Topics include**:

- Pricing model selection frameworks
- Per-seat licensing strategies
- Usage-based billing implementation
- Tiered and volume pricing
- Pricing page optimisation
- Price increase strategies

---

### Marketplace Monetisation

Platform-specific content for developers building on Trello, Miro, and similar ecosystems. Covers the unique challenges of marketplace billing: platform guidelines, user expectations, pricing norms, and technical integration.

**Topics include**:

- Trello Power-Up billing setup
- Miro plugin monetisation
- Marketplace pricing benchmarks
- Freemium conversion strategies
- Platform-specific entitlements

---

### Billing Operations

Technical and operational content about running billing infrastructure. Covers webhooks, testing, failed payments, subscription lifecycle, and system reliability.

**Topics include**:

- Webhook implementation patterns
- Billing integration testing
- Failed payment recovery
- Subscription state management
- Dunning and retention
- Invoice and receipt handling

---

### Product Updates

Announcements and deep-dives on Salable features. Educates users on new capabilities while demonstrating platform value to prospects.

**Topics include**:

- New feature announcements
- Feature deep-dive tutorials
- Migration guides
- Best practices with Salable features

## Publishing Cadence

**Frequency**: 2-3 articles per week (34 total over 12 weeks)

**Schedule**:

- **Tuesday**: Core educational content (startup guides, thought leadership)
- **Thursday**: Technical or platform-specific content (marketplace guides, beta features)
- **Friday** (weeks 1-8): Thought leadership

**Time of Publication**: 9:00 AM GMT for maximum reach across US and European audiences.

## Distribution Channels

### Primary Channels

**Blog (blog.salable.app)**
Home for all long-form content. Optimised for SEO with proper metadata, schema markup, and internal linking strategy.

**Newsletter**
Weekly digest highlighting new content, curated links, and exclusive insights. Drives return traffic and builds direct relationship with audience.

### Secondary Channels

**Twitter/X (@salaborhq)**
Thread breakdowns of key articles. Engagement with pricing and billing conversations. Quick takes on industry news.

**LinkedIn**
Professional positioning for founder and PM personas. Article excerpts with commentary. Company page updates for product announcements.

**Dev.to**
Technical content republished with canonical links. Targets engineering audience with practical tutorials and implementation guides.

**Reddit**
Selective sharing in relevant subreddits (r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur, r/webdev). Focus on genuinely helpful content, not promotion.

### Content Repurposing

Each long-form article generates:

- 3-5 Twitter/X posts
- 1 LinkedIn article excerpt
- 1 newsletter feature
- Code snippets for GitHub Gists (where applicable)
- Pull quotes for social graphics

## Editorial Guidelines

All content follows the Salable Marketing Content Style Guide (`claude/write-marketing-content.md`), which establishes:

- Conversational but authoritative voice
- Prose-first approach (lists support narrative, not replace it)
- Story spine structure for longer pieces
- Chicago Manual of Style formatting
- Direct address using "you"
- Active voice as default
