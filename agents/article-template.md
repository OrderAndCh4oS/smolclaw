# Article Template

Use this template for all article entries in content-type documents. Each article should follow this structure to ensure consistency across the marketing content strategy.

---

## Article Entry Format

```markdown
### [Article Number]. [Article Title]

**Synopsis**
[2-3 sentences describing what the article covers and its main argument]

**Lead Intro**
[Opening paragraph that hooks the reader - use narrative style, establish the problem or opportunity, make the reader want to continue]

**Target Audience**
[Primary persona: SaaS Founder | Marketplace Developer | Engineering Lead | Product Manager]

**Key Takeaway**
[Single most important insight the reader should remember]

**Salable Hook**
[How this article positions Salable - either a specific feature/capability being promoted, or "Demonstrates expertise in [topic]" for thought leadership pieces]

**Supporting Material**

- [Internal link to Salable documentation]
- [External authoritative source]
- [Additional research or examples]

**Estimated Word Count**: [1,200-2,500 words depending on depth]

**Content Pillar**: [Pricing Mastery | Marketplace Monetisation | Billing Operations | Product Updates]

---
```

## Field Guidelines

### Title

- Use title case following Chicago Manual of Style
- Make it specific and benefit-oriented
- Avoid clickbait; promise what the article delivers

### Synopsis

- Summarise the core argument in 2-3 sentences
- Include the "why it matters" element
- Should work as an internal planning document

### Lead Intro

- Write a complete opening paragraph (3-5 sentences)
- Use narrative structure: establish context, introduce tension, hint at resolution
- Make the reader feel this article was written for them
- Avoid starting with "In this article..." or similar meta-language

### Target Audience

Choose the primary persona:

- **SaaS Founder**: Building/scaling a subscription business, makes strategic decisions
- **Marketplace Developer**: Building apps for Trello, Miro, or similar platforms
- **Engineering Lead**: Responsible for billing integration, technical implementation
- **Product Manager**: Defining pricing strategy, feature packaging

### Key Takeaway

- One sentence capturing the essential insight
- Should be memorable and actionable
- If the reader remembers only this, the article succeeded

### Salable Hook

Clarify the marketing angle for each article:

- **Feature promotion**: "Promotes [specific Salable feature]" - links directly to a capability
- **Expertise demonstration**: "Demonstrates expertise in [topic]" - positions Salable as thought leader
- **Problem-solution**: "Positions Salable as solution for [pain point]" - sets up the need

Examples:

- "Promotes Salable's tiered pricing configuration"
- "Demonstrates expertise in SaaS pricing strategy"
- "Positions Salable as solution for billing complexity"

### Supporting Material

Include 2-4 links per article:

- At least one internal Salable doc (prefer beta.salable.app/docs)
- At least one external authoritative source
- Real examples, case studies, or research where relevant

## Example Entry

### 1. Your First Subscription Product: From Zero to Revenue in an Afternoon

**Synopsis**
Most developers overthink their first billing implementation, spending weeks on edge cases before earning their first dollar. This guide walks through launching a complete subscription product in hours, not weeks, by focusing on the critical path and deferring complexity until you have real customers.

**Lead Intro**
You've built something people want to pay for. Maybe it's a SaaS tool that's been running free while you validated the concept, or perhaps you're starting fresh with a clear monetisation strategy. Either way, you're facing the same question every developer confronts: how do you actually charge people? The billing landscape is littered with engineers who spent months building custom systems, only to discover Stripe's API had evolved while they coded. There's a better path. In the next few hours, you'll have a working subscription product, complete with checkout, entitlements, and payment processing.

**Target Audience**
SaaS Founder

**Key Takeaway**
Launch with a single plan and flat-rate pricing; complexity is a feature you add after customers tell you they need it.

**Salable Hook**
Positions Salable as the fastest path from idea to revenue; promotes Quick Start Guide as proof of simplicity

**Supporting Material**

- [Salable Quick Start Guide](https://beta.salable.app/docs/quick-start)
- [Stripe's Billing Best Practices](https://stripe.com/docs/billing/subscriptions/overview)
- [Indie Hackers Revenue Milestones](https://www.indiehackers.com/products)

**Estimated Word Count**: 1,800 words

**Content Pillar**: Billing Operations
