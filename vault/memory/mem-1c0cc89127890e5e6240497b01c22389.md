SYNTHESIS NOTE: The 'missing middle' in SaaS pricing refers to the gap between flat-rate or simplistic pricing (common for early-stage products) and sophisticated, value-based/usage-based pricing (used by more mature SaaS companies). Authoritative sources (OpenView, Baremetrics, L.E.K., and Tomasz Tunguz) agree on the following key points:
- Most SaaS start with flat or simplistic per-user pricing, but as products mature and customer/user data accrues, they shift toward value-linked or usage-based models.
- The transition requires (1) segmenting customers by usage/value, (2) identifying value drivers via data, (3) running pricing experiments (A/B, pilots), and (4) tracking willingness to pay and outcome metrics.
- Mature SaaS update pricing more frequently and bring pricing expertise in-house, using analytics tools to iterate toward value-based pricing.
- Hybrid pricing models (e.g., base subscription plus usage fee) are a waystation in this evolution.
- Transition is enabled by increased confidence/data on customer outcomes, allowing companies to align pricing with value delivered.
The strongest evidence comes from OpenView's benchmarks and Baremetrics' practical KPIs, while qualitative transitions are covered by Tunguz (strategy) and L.E.K. (outcome-based)
