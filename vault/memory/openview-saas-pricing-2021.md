#reference #pricing #customer research #openview #value-based

OpenView Partners (2021): Analysis of 2,200 SaaS companies finds only 4% score 'Excellent' on pricing capability; 44% score 'Failing' (little or no pricing process). Only 39% use value-based pricing (rest use judgement, competitor copying, or cost-plus). Customer research is largely absent—only 6% have done sophisticated studies on buyer needs/willingness to pay, 45% have done 'cursory' research; even among later-stage companies, only 17% have done in-depth WTP research. Regular pricing updates (at least annually, quarterly is optimal) tie to 25% higher ARR growth. Value-based pricing use correlates with larger deal size and better pricing alignment to customer outcomes. SaaS pricing should iterate with customer learning rather than copy competitors; pilot testing and customer feedback are underutilized for de-risking pricing changes.
