#reference #pricing #cta #ux #conversion

Nielsen Norman Group (Aurora Harley, Kim Flaherty, 2017) article 'Get Started' Stops Users: Generic calls-to-action (CTAs) like 'Get Started' on homepages and pricing pages can mislead and frustrate users, especially newcomers. Users often expect such CTAs to provide more info about the product or company, but instead they are pushed into sign-up flows or quizzes without context. This causes users to lose trust and abandon the site. Research with user studies and eyetracking showed that prominent, ambiguous CTAs caused users to stop looking for additional information, believing they needed to commit before learning more. Best practice: ensure pricing pages have specific, clear CTAs that reflect what will happen next and provide enough detail to build visitor trust before requesting commitment.
