#reference #pricing #cta #ux #trust

Nielsen Norman Group (nngroup.com), 'Show Prices for Common Scenarios' (2019): Best practice for pricing pages includes transparency about pricing and using CTAs that ground users' expectations. When pricing is complex, showing representative use cases and clear, scenario-based CTAs (e.g., 'See your price', 'Calculate your plan') helps reduce friction. Users are more likely to engage further (convert) when the initial action is clearly tied to exploring real outcomes, not forced commitment. Clear, outcome-oriented CTAs increase trust and encourage users to act.
