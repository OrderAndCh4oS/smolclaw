#journal #pricing #customer research #synthesis

Key synthesis: The strongest evidence from industry research (OpenView, ProfitWell/Patrick Campbell, Monetizely), consultancies (Simon-Kucher), and academic studies (Journal of the Association for Consumer Research, Michigan Journal of Economics) shows that sustainable SaaS pricing hinges on rigorous, ongoing customer research. Companies that collect segment-level data on willingness to pay, perceived feature value, and value metrics—rather than copying competitors—see consistently higher revenue growth, pricing power, and customer retention. Methods like value metric analysis, Van Westendorp, and MaxDiff reveal what customers genuinely value. Just 4-6% of SaaS firms conduct advanced pricing research today, but those that do reprice more frequently and outperform by 25-30% in growth and margin. The expert consensus: competitor benchmarking is necessary but insufficient—sustainable pricing demands learning directly from customers and closing the loop continuously as the product and market mature.
