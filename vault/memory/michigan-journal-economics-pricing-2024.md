#reference #pricing #customer research #pricing psychology

Michigan Journal of Economics (2024): Reviews academic research on pricing psychology, showing consumers anchor on initial price cues and assign elevated value when prices transparently reflect unique advantages and customer outcomes—insight reinforcing the need for customer-focused pricing. Survey/case evidence supports the claim that pricing reflecting customer-perceived utility and segmentation is more defensible/sustainable than competitor-based strategies.
