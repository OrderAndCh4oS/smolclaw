#reference #pricing #customer research #behavioral economics

Journal of the Association for Consumer Research (2021, vol. 6): Special issue on behavioral pricing highlights that consumer willingness to pay can far exceed classic economic predictions due to social/image motives and fairness considerations—reinforcing the importance of direct customer research over market-level assumptions when setting price. Research on novel pricing mechanisms (like pay-what-you-want) demonstrates customer psychology and fairness signals drive higher sustainable price points than pure competition-based logic would suggest.
